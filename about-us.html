#First stage

The first stage of training is introduction. In an artistic way, the material that is set for the relevant lesson cycle is presented. A relaxed situation is created, resembling an incident from everyday life, in which the grammatical and lexical structures, as well as the atmosphere and the theme of the relevant new lesson, are quite purposefully woven.

#Second stage

The second step consists in conducting the so-called "concert sessions," which help to memorize the material. They represent an expressive reading of the teaching material by the teacher against the background of specially selected classical music, which in the first session is intoned in order to emphasize the individual units of the language, and then is at a normal speed and rather conveys the main meaning of the text. In the first part, the students can follow the text and the translation, while in the second part, they just listen and enjoy the music that is playing.

#Third stage

The third stage is the development of the already presented material in the form of cheerful reading, games, theater, and dance. Through the pleasant, relaxed atmosphere and the spontaneous expression of the participants in the communication, language barriers are removed, creativity is stimulated, and the emotional tone of the course participants is raised. At the same time, a lot of the information that was already given in a passive way is turned on. 

#Forth stage

The fourth stage, or the presentation of the course participants, is both a reinforcement of knowledge and an occasion for individual expression. At this stage, speaking skills are further stimulated and developed, and good feedback is also given to the teacher for a job well done. This is also the time when each participant can judge their own performance, which is a good way to keep them going.
